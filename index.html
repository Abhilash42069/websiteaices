    About

    AICES 2022

    The conference aims to provide a forum for researchers, practitioners and professionals from the industry and the academia to share their research trends, findings, and results in the multidisciplinary field of intelligent computing. Various applications of intelligent computing in earth and environment science, biomedical engineering, and information technology will be covered. Topics like geospatial analysis, weather and climate forecasting, biomedical imaging and signal processing, internet of things, machine learning and deep learning applications, biomedical instrumentation, medical imaging physics etc. are in the scope of conference. The Conference will highlight diverse perspectives to interested geologists, meteorologists, biomedical engineers, software engineers and computer professionals, medical professionals, biotechnologists, pharmaceutical experts, sustainability researchers etc. and will also provide a platform for potential knowledge exchange on recent trends, theories and practices in the field of intelligent computing. Intelligent computing is one of the emerging fields that can add new and better application in a wide range of sectors like healthcare, service sector, and processing industry to name some. The conference will discuss advancements that will enable the researchers to develop improved approaches for solving science and engineering problems.

    Research articles are invited in the following areas of interest, but not limited to:
    Track-I

    Intelligent computing in earth and environmental sciences

    • Geospatial analysis
    • Environment
    • Weather and climate forecasting
    • Atmospheric science
    • Natural hazards
    • Medical Geology
    • Mineral prospecting and exploration
    • Hydrogeology


    Track-II

    Computing Technology

    • Big Data/ Data Mining/ Text Mining/ Time series Mining /Data Informatics
    • Image Processing/Pattern Recognition/Multimedia
    • Geographical Information Systems
    • Machine Learning / Machine Intelligence / Artificial Intelligence
    • Soft Computing (AI, Neural Networks, Fuzzy)
    • Internet of Things


    Track-III

    Intelligent computing in Biomedical Engineering

    • nCOVID-19 research
    • Health informatics
    • Biomedical Signal Processing
    • Medical Image Processing
    • Biomedical Instrumentation
    • Non-Invasive Techniques
    • Medical Imaging Physics
    • AI & Machine learning in health
    • Cognitive Science/Engineering
    • Brain Computer Interfaces
